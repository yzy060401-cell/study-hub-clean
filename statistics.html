Week 1

Sampling & Describing Data

抽样与描述数据

Key Concepts

  • Population and sample ｜ 总体与样本
  • Statistic and parameter ｜ 统计量与总体参数
  • Mean and median ｜ 均值与中位数
  • Percentiles ｜ 百分位数
  • Standard deviation ｜ 标准差
  • Standard error ｜ 标准误
  • 95% confidence interval ｜ 95% 置信区间
  • Sampling bias ｜ 抽样偏差

Core Logic

  • We usually want to learn about a population, but in practice we only observe a sample. ｜ 我们通常想了解总体，但实际中只能观察样本。
  • A sample statistic is used to estimate an unknown population value. ｜ 样本统计量用于估计未知的总体参数。
  • Uncertainty remains because different random samples would produce slightly different answers. ｜ 由于不同随机样本会给出不同结果，所以估计总带有不确定性。
  • Bigger samples often reduce random uncertainty, but they do not automatically fix bad sampling. ｜ 更大的样本通常能降低随机误差，但不能自动修复错误抽样。

Important Distinction

  • Standard deviation describes spread in the data. ｜ 标准差描述数据本身的离散程度。
  • Standard error describes uncertainty in the sample estimate. ｜ 标准误描述样本估计值的不确定性。
  • Mean is sensitive to extreme values; median is often more robust. ｜ 均值更容易受极端值影响，中位数通常更稳健。
  • A random-looking collection is not necessarily a valid random sample. ｜ 看起来“随便拿的”数据，并不等于真正的随机抽样。

Exam Thinking

In Week 1, a strong answer does not just define mean or confidence interval. It explains whether the sample is suitable for answering the question in the first place.

第1周的好答案不只是定义均值或置信区间， 而是先判断这个样本本身是否适合回答题目中的问题。

Week 2

Correlation, Regression & Prediction

相关、回归与预测

Key Concepts

  • Correlation coefficient ｜ 相关系数
  • Scatter plot ｜ 散点图
  • Regression line ｜ 回归线
  • Slope coefficient ｜ 斜率系数
  • Intercept ｜ 截距
  • R² ｜ 判定系数
  • Prediction ｜ 预测
  • Outlier ｜ 离群值

Core Logic

  • Correlation tells us whether two variables move together. ｜ 相关性告诉我们两个变量是否一起变化。
  • Regression adds a predictive model and estimates the average relationship between X and Y. ｜ 回归进一步建立预测模型，估计 X 与 Y 的平均关系。
  • R² measures how much variation in the outcome is explained by the predictor in the model. ｜ R² 衡量模型中的解释变量解释了结果变量多少变异。
  • Good analysis should start with plotting the data, not jumping straight to output tables. ｜ 好的分析应先看图，而不是直接跳到回归结果表。

Important Distinction

  • Correlation is not the same as causation. ｜ 相关不等于因果。
  • A significant slope does not prove an intervention would work. ｜ 斜率显著不代表干预一定有效。
  • Prediction quality and causal interpretation are different questions. ｜ 预测能力和因果解释是两类不同问题。
  • Extrapolating far beyond the observed data is risky. ｜ 超出样本范围过多地外推是有风险的。

Exam Thinking

When interpreting a regression, mention direction, size, uncertainty, and fit. Do not only say whether the coefficient is positive or negative.

解释回归结果时，要同时谈方向、大小、不确定性和拟合度， 不要只说系数是正还是负。

Week 3

Inference & Hypothesis Testing

统计推断与假设检验

Key Concepts

  • Null hypothesis ｜ 原假设
  • Alternative hypothesis ｜ 备择假设
  • p-value ｜ p值
  • Statistical significance ｜ 统计显著性
  • Confidence interval ｜ 置信区间
  • Standard error ｜ 标准误
  • Difference in means ｜ 均值差异
  • Evidence vs noise ｜ 证据与随机波动

Core Logic

  • Inference uses sample data to say something about a wider population. ｜ 推断统计用样本去推测更大的总体。
  • Hypothesis testing asks whether an observed difference could plausibly be due to chance alone. ｜ 假设检验判断观察到的差异是否可能只是随机波动造成。
  • Confidence intervals help show both uncertainty and the range of plausible values. ｜ 置信区间同时展示不确定性和可能的数值范围。
  • Statistical decision-making is about uncertainty, not certainty. ｜ 统计决策处理的是不确定性，而不是绝对确定。

Important Distinction

  • Statistically significant does not automatically mean practically important. ｜ 统计显著不自动等于现实意义很大。
  • A p-value is not the probability that the null hypothesis is true. ｜ p值并不是“原假设为真”的概率。
  • Rejecting the null is not the same as proving a theory forever. ｜ 拒绝原假设不等于永久证明某个理论。
  • Wide intervals mean high uncertainty even when point estimates look interesting. ｜ 即使点估计看起来很显眼，区间很宽仍说明不确定性高。

Exam Thinking

A strong inference answer explains what the interval or p-value means in context, instead of treating significance as a magic yes-or-no stamp.

好的推断题答案会结合情境解释 p 值或置信区间的含义， 而不是把“显著”当成机械的二元判断。

Week 4

Causality, Confounding & Experimentation

因果、混杂与实验设计

Key Concepts

  • Causality ｜ 因果关系
  • Confounding ｜ 混杂因素
  • Reverse causality ｜ 反向因果
  • Omitted variable ｜ 遗漏变量
  • Randomised controlled trial ｜ 随机对照试验
  • Treatment and control ｜ 处理组与对照组
  • Experimentation ｜ 实验设计
  • Identification ｜ 因果识别

Core Logic

  • Observing that X and Y move together does not tell us whether X causes Y. ｜ 观察到 X 与 Y 一起变化，并不能说明 X 导致 Y。
  • Confounders can create misleading relationships by affecting both variables. ｜ 混杂变量会同时影响两者，从而制造误导性的关系。
  • Reverse causality means the direction of cause may run the other way. ｜ 反向因果意味着真正的因果方向可能相反。
  • Randomisation helps balance hidden factors and makes causal claims more credible. ｜ 随机化有助于平衡隐藏因素，使因果推断更可信。

Important Distinction

  • Controlling for more variables is not always the same as identifying causality. ｜ 控制更多变量并不等于真的识别了因果。
  • Prediction models and causal models answer different questions. ｜ 预测模型和因果模型回答的是不同问题。
  • An experiment estimates what happened because of the intervention, not just what happened after it. ｜ 实验估计的是“因为干预而发生了什么”，而不只是“干预后发生了什么”。

Exam Thinking

In Week 4, always ask: what is the counterfactual? In other words, what would have happened without the treatment?

第4周做题时一定要问： 反事实是什么？也就是如果没有这个干预，本来会发生什么？

Week 5

Residuals, Transformations & Interaction Effects

残差、变量变换与交互效应

Key Concepts

  • Residuals ｜ 残差
  • Fitted values ｜ 拟合值
  • Nonlinearity ｜ 非线性
  • Transformation ｜ 变量变换
  • Homoskedasticity ｜ 同方差性
  • Heteroskedasticity ｜ 异方差性
  • Interaction term ｜ 交互项
  • Effect modification ｜ 效应修饰

Core Logic

  • Residuals help us check whether a linear model is missing important structure. ｜ 残差可以帮助我们检查线性模型是否遗漏了重要结构。
  • A curved residual pattern often signals that the model form is wrong. ｜ 残差出现明显弯曲，往往意味着模型形式不对。
  • Transforming variables can make relationships easier to model. ｜ 对变量做变换有时能让关系更适合建模。
  • Interaction terms allow one variable’s effect to depend on another variable. ｜ 交互项允许一个变量的影响取决于另一个变量。

Important Distinction

  • Confounding and effect modification are not the same. ｜ 混杂和效应修饰不是一回事。
  • Noise around the line is normal; a systematic pattern in residuals is more serious. ｜ 拟合线周围有随机噪音是正常的，但残差出现系统性模式更值得警惕。
  • Better fit is not just about higher R²; the model also needs to make sense diagnostically. ｜ 更好的拟合不只是更高的 R²，还要诊断上说得通。

Exam Thinking

If a question shows residual plots, do not ignore them. They are usually telling you whether the chosen model is appropriate.

如果题目给了残差图，不要忽略。 它往往就是在告诉你当前模型到底合不合适。

Week 6

Logistic Regression & Binary Outcomes

Logistic 回归与二元结果变量

Key Concepts

  • Binary outcome ｜ 二元结果
  • Probability ｜ 概率
  • Odds ｜ 胜算
  • Odds ratio ｜ 胜算比
  • Risk and risk ratio ｜ 风险与风险比
  • Logistic regression ｜ Logistic 回归
  • Threshold ｜ 判定阈值
  • Classification ｜ 分类

Core Logic

  • When the outcome is yes/no, linear regression is usually not the best model. ｜ 当结果变量只有是/否时，线性回归通常不是最合适的模型。
  • Logistic regression models the probability of an event while keeping predicted values between 0 and 1. ｜ Logistic 回归建模的是事件发生概率，并保证预测值在 0 到 1 之间。
  • Binary outcomes are common in health research, such as recurrence, success, or disease status. ｜ 医疗研究中二元结果很常见，例如复发、成功、是否患病。
  • Interpretation needs care because odds and probabilities are related but not identical. ｜ 解释时要小心，因为胜算和概率有关，但并不相同。

Important Distinction

  • Probability is not the same as odds. ｜ 概率不等于胜算。
  • Odds ratio is not the same as risk ratio. ｜ 胜算比不等于风险比。
  • Using the correct model depends on the outcome type, not personal preference. ｜ 选模型应根据结果变量类型，而不是个人偏好。

Exam Thinking

In Week 6, one of the most common marks is for simply identifying the right model from the outcome variable type.

第6周最常见的得分点之一， 就是先根据结果变量的类型识别出正确的统计模型。

Week 7

Survival Analysis

生存分析 / 时间到事件分析

Key Concepts

  • Time-to-event data ｜ 时间到事件数据
  • Event ｜ 事件
  • Censoring ｜ 删失
  • Survival probability ｜ 生存概率
  • Survival curve ｜ 生存曲线
  • Risk set ｜ 风险集
  • Discrete-time survival model ｜ 离散时间生存模型
  • Follow-up ｜ 随访

Core Logic

  • For survival data, timing matters, not just whether the event happened. ｜ 对生存数据来说，不只是事件是否发生，发生的时间也很重要。
  • Censored observations still contain useful information up to the point they leave observation. ｜ 被删失的个体在退出观察之前仍然提供了有用信息。
  • Survival methods are designed for unequal follow-up and incomplete event timing. ｜ 生存分析就是为不等长随访和不完整事件时间而设计的。
  • The unit of analysis often changes when preparing data for survival models. ｜ 为生存模型整理数据时，分析单位常常会发生变化。

Important Distinction

  • Binary outcome analysis ignores timing; survival analysis uses timing. ｜ 二元结果分析忽略时间信息，而生存分析会利用时间信息。
  • Censoring does not mean “nothing happened”; it means the event time is not fully observed. ｜ 删失不代表“什么都没发生”，而是事件时间没有被完整观察到。
  • Dropping censored cases can seriously bias analysis. ｜ 直接删掉删失个体会严重偏倚分析。

Exam Thinking

In Week 7, always check whether the question is really about a binary outcome or about time until an outcome.

第7周做题时一定要先判断： 题目问的是一个二元结果，还是“多久之后发生结果”。

Week 8

Data Visualisation & Tableau Thinking

数据可视化与 Tableau 思维

Key Concepts

  • Data visualisation ｜ 数据可视化
  • Chart choice ｜ 图表选择
  • Dimension and measure ｜ 维度与度量
  • Line chart ｜ 折线图
  • Histogram ｜ 直方图
  • Box plot ｜ 箱线图
  • Dashboard ｜ 仪表板
  • Storytelling ｜ 数据讲故事

Core Logic

  • Good visualisation helps people think, compare, and decide. ｜ 好的可视化能帮助人理解、比较和做决策。
  • Different charts answer different questions, such as trend, distribution, or comparison. ｜ 不同图表适合回答不同问题，例如趋势、分布或比较。
  • Visual design should support interpretation rather than distract from it. ｜ 图形设计应服务于解释，而不是喧宾夺主。
  • Tableau work is not only technical; it is also about communication. ｜ Tableau 不只是技术操作，也涉及表达与沟通。

Important Distinction

  • A chart can look attractive but still be misleading. ｜ 图表可以看起来很漂亮，但依然可能误导。
  • Exploration and presentation are related but not identical goals. ｜ 数据探索与最终展示有关联，但目标并不完全一样。
  • Choosing the wrong chart can hide the most important pattern. ｜ 选错图表可能会掩盖最重要的模式。

Exam Thinking

In Week 8, a common question is not “which chart do you like” but “which chart best matches the analytical goal.”

第8周常考的不是“你喜欢哪个图”， 而是“哪个图最适合当前的分析目标”。

Week 9

Introduction to Data Science

数据科学导论

Key Concepts

  • Data science pipeline ｜ 数据科学流程
  • Insight ｜ 数据洞察
  • Prediction ｜ 预测
  • Prescription ｜ 决策建议
  • Features and label ｜ 特征与标签
  • Train / test split ｜ 训练集 / 测试集
  • Data leakage ｜ 数据泄漏
  • Baseline ｜ 基线模型

Core Logic

  • Data science turns raw records into insights, predictions, and actions. ｜ 数据科学把原始记录转化为洞察、预测和行动。
  • Earlier statistical ideas still matter: uncertainty, modelling, and careful interpretation. ｜ 前面学过的统计思想依然重要，比如不确定性、建模和谨慎解释。
  • Model performance should be judged on unseen data, not only on the training sample. ｜ 模型表现应在未见过的数据上评估，而不只是训练样本内。
  • The goal is usually decision support, not prediction for its own sake. ｜ 最终目标通常是支持决策，而不是为了预测而预测。

Important Distinction

  • Prediction is not the same as explanation. ｜ 预测不等于解释。
  • Prediction is not the same as causality. ｜ 预测不等于因果。
  • A model that memorises training data may fail in real-world use. ｜ 只会记住训练数据的模型，到了真实场景可能会失败。

Exam Thinking

Week 9 often rewards students who can connect old statistics ideas to the broader data science workflow.

第9周常奖励那些能把前面的统计知识 与更完整的数据科学流程连接起来的学生。

Week 10

Machine Learning, Decision Trees & Interpretability

机器学习、决策树与可解释性

Key Concepts

  • Machine learning ｜ 机器学习
  • Decision tree ｜ 决策树
  • Split ｜ 分裂规则
  • Node and leaf ｜ 节点与叶节点
  • Overfitting ｜ 过拟合
  • Depth ｜ 树深度
  • Interpretability ｜ 可解释性
  • Random forest ｜ 随机森林

Core Logic

  • A decision tree predicts using a sequence of if-then rules. ｜ 决策树通过一连串 if-then 规则进行预测。
  • Trees are attractive because they are relatively easy to explain to non-technical audiences. ｜ 决策树的优势之一是相对容易向非技术受众解释。
  • Very deep trees can fit training data too closely and perform poorly on new data. ｜ 过深的树会过度贴合训练数据，在新数据上反而表现变差。
  • Interpretability matters when models are used in health and management decisions. ｜ 当模型用于医疗和管理决策时，可解释性非常重要。

Important Distinction

  • A predictive rule is not a causal rule. ｜ 预测规则不等于因果规则。
  • A more flexible model is not always a better model for communication or governance. ｜ 更灵活的模型并不一定更适合沟通或治理。
  • Readable models can still be wrong if trained badly or evaluated badly. ｜ 就算模型很“可读”，训练和评估做不好也照样会错。

Exam Thinking

In Week 10, do not describe a tree rule as proof that changing a variable will change the outcome.

第10周做题时，不要把决策树中的规则 误写成“改变这个变量就一定会改变结果”的因果证明。
